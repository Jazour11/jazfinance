Compound Interest

Compound interest is a very important concept when it comes to financial wellness. In simple terms this is the
interest earned on previous interest.This means when you invest money, and it earns interest the next time it
earns interest it will earn interest on the original amount invested plus of top of the previous interest which
causes it to compound over periods of time. This is same for debts except the interest is not going to you, its
coming from you, which is why it is important to keep your debt as low as possible in order to maximize your
wealth.For example if you purchase a home for $ 200,000 on a 30 year fixed rate mortgage at four percent interest
you will actually pay about $ 340,000 by the end of the mortgage which means the bank will will have an roi of
about 70 percent over the life of the loan before all of their fees, and and assuming you dont slip up at all along
the way. If you were able to opt for a 15 year mortgage on that same $ 200,000 dollar home you would only pay about
$ 301,000 which saves you $ 39,000 in interest expense that you can invest. If you invested this 39,000 that you
saved for 15 years that you would have left on your 30 year note, at the s&p 500s average return of 10 percent
compounded annually that $39,000 dollars has now become $162,912.68 saved all because you minimized your time
spent in debt. This is why learning about compound interest is really cool, and can help you to grow your net
worth, and make wise financial descisions. Please play around with the calculator I built in order to help you
dream, and help to plan for your future.

Emergency Fund

This is one of the most important financial topics to help increase financial wellness. Many people are realizing with the
recent pandemic that emergency funds are absolutely vital in todays economy. In the past alot of people would spend their
whole life at the same job, and that is much less common today. Job security is not the same as it was then making the
emergency funds a vital financial concept for people to understand,and set aside money for. Most fincancial resources have
always reccomended 3-6 months of expenses as a proper emergency fund, but with the recent pandemic stretching over 1 year.
I think that it is important that when we have extra money we work on getting our emergency fund up to about 6-12months of
expenses. An emergency fund is only to be used for emergencies. This is not to be used because you overspent as a safety
net. It is for unexpected emergencies such as medicial emergencies, major car or applicance repairs, etc. The emergency
fund can be a very important tool to break credit card, and personal debt borrowing because when you have an emergency
that causes you to think irrationally you will have the money you need which will help you make wise descisions. Please
come up with a budget that includes all of your expenses, and input the expenses below to calculate a proper emergency
fund. When you see how much you need for your emergency fund, it may seem daunting, but you can attack this overtime. I
think it is important to get this in place before investing, because it will allow you to not treat your investments as
an emergency fund, so that you can hold longterm, in order for your investment to grow, and accumulate the compound
interest that we talked about before.
